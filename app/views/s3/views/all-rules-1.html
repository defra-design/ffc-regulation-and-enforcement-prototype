Test for salmonella: rules summary

Laying hens

Register premises where you keep 50 hens or more

Tell APHA or DAERA when you expect 250 or more hens to arrive on your holding

Test for salmonella if you are a commercial egg producer and keep 350 or more hens

Take samples from:

  • chicks on the day they arrive from a hatchery
  • pullets before they move to the laying unit
  • adult hens every 15 weeks while they lay (start when they are 22 to 26 weeks old)

Send samples to a UK approved laboratory

Label each sample with the information required by law

If a flock tests positive for salmonella, you must not sell the eggs as Class A

Keep salmonella test results, hen movement and medicine records for at least 2 years

Allow APHA to take official samples once a year (you will have to pay)

Chicken breeders

Register hatcheries or premises that have 50 birds or more

Test for salmonella if you keep 250 or more breeding hens

Take samples from:

  • chicks on the day they arrive from a hatchery
  • young birds at 4 weeks old
  • young birds 2 weeks before they move to the laying unit
  • adult breeding birds every 3 weeks while they lay

Label each sample with the information required by law

Send samples to a UK approved laboratory

Use a vet or official to take samples if you export hatching eggs or you supply eggs or day-old chicks to someone who does

Tell APHA or DAERA when you expect 250 or more breeding hens to arrive on your holding

Keep salmonella test results, hen movement and medicine records for at least 2 years

Allow APHA to take official samples twice in the lifetime of the flock, at the beginning and end of the lay period

Broiler chickens

Register premises that have 50 birds or more

Take samples from your flocks within the three weeks before they are killed

Label each sample with the information required by law

Send samples to a UK approved laboratory on the day you collect them

Send the NCP salmonella test result and the sample date to the slaughterhouse

If a flock tests positive for salmonella, you must clean and disinfect your premises, follow restrictions and get a licence from APHA or DAERA to move equipment, animals or animal products on or off your holding

Keep salmonella test results, hen movement and medicine records for at least 2 years

Allow APHA to take official samples

Follow other food hygiene legislation and food chain information from the Food Standards Agency
