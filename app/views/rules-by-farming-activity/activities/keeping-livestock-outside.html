Manage livestock and feeders to prevent soil erosion

What you must do to prevent livestock from causing diffuse pollution and soil erosion on your farm.

Contents

  1. Legal requirements
  2. Best practice when you choose locations for livestock feeders
  3. Best practice to prevent livestock causing soil loss

Legal requirements

You must not place livestock feeders:

  • within 10m of inland freshwaters or coastal waters
  • within 50m of a spring, well or borehole
  • where risk factors like slope, soil conditions or nearby water sources or land drains mean there’s a significant risk of pollution

Best practice when you choose locations for livestock feeders

Record where you place livestock feeders on a farm map or plan.

Best practice to prevent livestock causing soil loss

Take reasonable precautions to prevent erosion and runoff over a single area of one or more hectares as a result of livestock. You should:

  • monitor the impact of livestock on your fields
  • move livestock regularly or if there’s evidence of soil loss or pollution from poaching next to inland or coastal waters
  • avoid poaching over a single stretch of agricultural land of at least 2 metres wide and 20 metres long
  • erect fencing around inland freshwaters or coastal waters
  • winter livestock on well-drained, level fields

Record any precautions you’ve taken to reduce the risk of compacting soil.
