Store and handle feed

To stop contamination and distribution errors:

  • keep storage areas and containers clean and dry, in particular those used to contain medicated and non-medicated feed, and use appropriate pest-control measures where necessary
  • store seed properly and in such a way that it is not accessible to animals
  • handle medicated and non-medicated feeds separately
  • make sure the on-farm feed distribution system ensures that the right feed is sent to the right destination and does not get contaminated from storage areas and equipment
  • periodically cleaning on-farm feed transport vehicles and feeding equipment, in particular when used to deliver and distribute medicated feed

Act on official controls

Act on results of official controls and sample analyses. Take appropriate remedial action when informed of problems identified during official controls.

Use feed additives, medicines and biocides correctly

Use as prescribed or as stated on the label. Follow instructions for dosage, how to apply and storage.

Make sure that the food you produce does not contain residues that are higher than the permitted maximum residue level for the pesticide or medicine used.

Take measures to stop diseases passing to humans through food

Take adequate measures to stop the introduction and spread of contagious diseases transmissible to humans through food. This includes:

  • abiding by the statutory herd testing for bovine tuberculosis and pre-movement TB testing of animals
  • compliance with any notice and instruction
  • not moving animals subject to movement restrictions
  • taking precautionary measures when introducing new animals
  • reporting suspected disease outbreaks to the competent authority

Keep records

You must keep records about:

  • vet medicines or other treatments given to your animals (include the treatment dates and withdrawal periods)
  • plant protection products and biocides
  • results of analyses carried out on samples taken that have importance for human or animal health
  • reports on checks on animals and animal products
  • any use of genetically modified seeds in feed production

These rules do not apply to producers selling small amounts of primary products to final consumers or to local shops selling directly to final consumers.
