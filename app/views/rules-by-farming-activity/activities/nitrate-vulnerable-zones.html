Nitrate Vulnerable Zones (NVZs) are areas designated as being at risk from pollution from agricultural nitrates. They cover about 55% of the land in England. You can help us reduce pollution in these areas.

Find out if your land is in an NVZ

The Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) reviews NVZs every 4 years to account for changes in nitrate concentrations. You can check for changes to the boundaries.

Find out if your land is an NVZ

Contact the Environment Agency if you have difficulty checking online or need a printed map.

What you can do if your land is designated as an NVZ

If NVZ boundaries are redrawn to include your land, you can appeal an NVZ decision.

Rules for all farmers who have land in an NVZ

Find out the nitrogen limit for the crops you grow and when you can apply more than these limits.

Make a fertilisation plan to record your use of nitrogen on crops and grassland.

Assess the risk of runoff

Keep records about your fertiliser use for at least 5 years

You may have to follow different rules if you apply fertiliser in greenhouses, glass houses, polytunnels or you apply nitrogen fertiliser on a low-intensity farm.

Rules for farming activities in NVZs

Spread manufactured fertilisers

Spread organic manures

Spread cattle, chicken or pig slurry with high readily available nitrogen

Spread certified green or certified green/food compost

Store organic manures

Follow best practice for nitrates

You must follow the Code of Good Agricultural Practice for nitrates if you claim agri-environment payments.

Inspections

The Environment Agency enforces the NVZ rules. Its officers select which farms to inspect. Officers will offer advice to help you comply with the NVZ rules.

Get advice

Contact the Farming Advice Service.
