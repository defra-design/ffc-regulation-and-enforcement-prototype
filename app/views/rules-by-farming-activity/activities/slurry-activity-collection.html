Storing manure, slurry or silage

Rules farmers and land managers must follow to store or build storage for manure, slurry and silage.

You must avoid causing pollution from your farming activities.

These are legal requirements to protect the environment. The Environment Agency enforces the rules around storing slurry and silage. They may offer advice and instructions to change how you deal with slurry or silage. You can be fined or prosecuted if you break the law.

Storing manure

You can store manure in a temporary field heap if it is ‘stackable’ and does not slump. You must follow extra rules when you store manure in areas at risk from agricultural nitrate pollution.

Keeping livestock outside

If you keep livestock outside, you must take measures to avoid water pollution. Also includes rules that limit where you can put animal feeders.
