Find out the rules that apply to your farming activities.

Manage land

On all land you must follow the rules and:

  • learn how to manage and dispose of waste
  • protect and maintain hedgerows
  • maintain watercourses
  • manage soils appropriately and avoid causing pollution as described in farming rules for water
  • for storing agricultural fuel oil

You must follow rules if you:

  • grow new woodland,fell trees or have to protect tree health
  • burn heather or grassland, you can only do this at certain times and may need a licence
  • have to protect scheduled monuments on your land
  • manage land with public rights of way, open access rights or in the coastal margin
  • manage or make use of common land
  • own or occupy sites of special scientific interest
  • plan on changing your land use you may need to apply for an Environmental Impact Assessment for rural land or forestry
  • discharge liquid effluent or waste water to surface water or the ground
  • produce or use sewage sludge in agriculture
  • spread waste on your land

Keep animals

If you keep animals, you must follow rules:

  • relating to animal welfare on and off your land and when transporting
  • to identify and track animals, including cattle, sheep, goats, pigs, deer, poultry
  • to control animal movements and making sure animal movements don’t affect soil quality
  • to protect walkers from cattle
  • on animal feed safety and hygiene
  • about animal medicines
  • and report notifiable animal diseases to the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA)
  • for storing slurry and silage, you need sufficient and appropriate storage facilities, and permission for those facilities
  • to dispose of fallen stock
  • to manage animal by-products
  • when farming pigs or poultry intensively

Grow plants

Growing any plants, if you:

  • use pesticides you must register as a user, follow the rules for storing, transporting and using pesticides, and have an up-to-dateintegrated pest management plan
  • use any fertilisers or manures you should have an up-to-date nutrient management plan, which is based on recent soil testing
  • are in a nitrate vulnerable zone your plans for using manure or fertiliser should take this into account
  • abstract water you may need a licence from the Environment Agency, and if you irrigate land you should be aware of restrictions
  • burn crop residues you should be aware of rules about restrictions on the places and times this is allowed

Trading with other countries

There are licences, rules and restrictions if you:

  • import or export goods
  • want to be certified as an organic farmer
  • breed animals
  • breed plants
