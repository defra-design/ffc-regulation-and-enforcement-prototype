Find out the rules that apply to your farming activities.

Keep livestock

If you keep livestock, you must follow rules to protect the health and welfare of your livestock and customers. Follow the rules:

  • to register land you use to keep livestock and register to keep poultry
  • relating to animal welfare on and off your land and when transporting them
  • to identify and trace cattle, sheep, goats, pigs and deer
  • breed animals
  • to produce safe to eat food
  • on animal feed safety and hygiene
  • about animal medicines
  • to report notifiable animal diseases to the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA)
  • when farming pigs or poultry intensively
  • to make sure animal movements don’t damage soil
  • to protect the public from cattle
  • for storing slurry and silage, you need sufficient and appropriate storage facilities, and permission for those facilities
  • to dispose of fallen stock
  • to manage animal by-products

Keep horses

Follow the rules for keeping horses or keeping horses commercially.

Grow crops

If you grow or work on crops, grass or plants, you must follow rules when you:

  • store, transport and use pesticides, you must also register as a user and plan before you apply
  • abstract water you may need a licence from the Environment Agency, and if you irrigate land you should be aware of restrictions
  • use any fertilisers or manures you should have an up-to-date nutrient management plan, which is based on recent soil testing
  • use sewage sludge on your land
  • spread waste on your land
  • burn crop residues you should be aware of rules about restrictions on the places and times this is allowed
  • breed plants

Manage land, water and boundaries

On all land you must follow the rules and:

  • manage soils appropriately and avoid causing water pollution as described in farming rules for water
  • maintain watercourses
  • manage land with public rights of way, open access rights or in the coastal margin
  • burn heather or grassland - you can only do this at certain times and may need a licence
  • check if you need to apply for an Environmental Impact Assessment before you change or improve rural land
  • find out when you can cut, trim, remove or work on a hedgerow

Protect landscape features

There are extra rules to protect local places or historic features. You must:

  • take care of sites of special scientific interest you own or occupy
  • protect scheduled monuments

Farming in nitrate vulnerable zones

There are extra rules if you farm in a nitrate vulnerable zone.

Change to organic farming

You must get certified as an organic farmer to call a food product organic.

Manage woodland

You must follow rules to:

  • grow new woodland
  • fell trees
  • protect tree health
  • check if you need to apply for an Environmental Impact Assessment to change the use of forestry land

Manage and dispose of oil and waste

Follow the rules to:

  • store oil and build new storage facilities
  • manage and dispose of waste and oil
  • discharge liquid effluent or waste water to surface water or the ground

Import or export goods

There are licences, rules and restrictions if you import or export goods and extra rules around importing live animals and animal products.

Cross compliance

The rules have not changed. To help you find rules, this guidance page summarises the rules for farming activities. The rules in cross compliance still apply and you must follow those rules on your holding to receive rural payments in full.
