Before you apply manure and when you must not apply it

To summarise the rules are:

  • plan before you apply organic manure to use only what the soil and crop needs
  • assess and avoid spreading manure if there is a significant risk of pollution

Do not apply manure:

  • to waterlogged, flooded or snow covered soil
  • if soil has been frozen for more than 12 hours in the previous 24
  • within 10 metres (m) of any inland or coastal waters
  • within 50m of a spring, well or borehole

Applying manure in a nitrate vulnerable zone

To summarise the rules are:

  • you can apply up to 170 kilograms per hectare of livestock manure in a year
  • do not use more than 250kg on a hectare in a year
  • use available standard values to work out how much nitrogen your livestock produces or is moved onto your farm
  • plan how much nitrogen you need to use on each crop in each field (including grass)
  • do not allow organic manure to enter rivers, ponds or other surface water
  • assess the risk of runoff
  • only spread manure on agricultural land that grows crops or grass
  • work manures into the soil as soon as practically possible
  • keep records for 5 years

Spreading poultry manure or slurry

Spreading poultry manure or liquid manure (such as sludge, pig or cattle slurries), you must:

  • only spread within certain dates
  • monitor the levels of potentially toxic elements (PTE) in slurry and in the soil
  • know PTE levels before you spread on your land
  • know what has already been spread on your land
  • not apply sludge to land with a pH of less than 5.0
  • plan when to use sludge to fit in with planting, grazing or harvesting
  • keep records about sludge test results, soil test results and how much you have used on each site
  • protect the environment and avoid public nuisance
  • make sure sludge doesn't run off onto roads or adjacent land or into rivers or waterways
  • stop spreading if it rains heavily enough to cause runoff
