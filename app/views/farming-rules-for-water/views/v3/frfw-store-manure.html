Siting your manure stores away from water can reduce the risk of nutrients leaching into drains and streams.

Rules for storing manure

You must not store manure:

  • within 10m of inland freshwaters or coastal waters
  • within 50m of a spring, well or borehole
  • where risk factors like soil condition, nearby waterways or drains mean there’s a significant risk of pollution

How to choose a site to store manure

To be legally compliant, you must check:

  • the distance of your store from surface waters, springs, wells or boreholes
  • the area around stored organic manures for potential pathways where runoff could flow into inland and coastal waters
  • if there is a significant risk of pollution from the location

How to avoid significant risk of pollution

Before you make a manure store you must assess pollution risks.

You must take into account the risks of runoff and soil erosion from these factors:

  • the angle of slopes, in particular if the angle is greater than 12 degrees
  • amount of ground cover
  • distance to inland freshwaters, coastal waters, wetlands, springs, wells or boreholes
  • presence and condition of land drains
  • soil type and condition

You must identify the risks that apply to your land and your activities.

Keep records

To be legally compliant, you need a written document that details:

  • where you’ve put your manure stores
  • risk factors in that area, for example, nearby waterways, field drains, springs, wells and boreholes
  • what you’ve done to reduce the risk
