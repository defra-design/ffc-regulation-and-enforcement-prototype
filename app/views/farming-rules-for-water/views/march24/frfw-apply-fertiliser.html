Rules for applying fertiliser

When you apply fertiliser, you must:

  • plan how much to apply to make sure you meet soil and crop needs and don’t lose nutrients into waterways
  • take into account the weather conditions and forecasts at the time
  • assess the risk of pollution

You must not apply fertiliser:

  • on waterlogged, flooded or snow covered soil
  • when soil has been frozen for more than 12 hours in the past 24 hours
  • if there is significant risk of causing pollution
  • within 2 metres (m) of inland freshwaters, coastal waters, a spring, well or borehole
  • within 2m of hedgerows

Best practice when you apply fertiliser

Before you apply fertiliser, you should take reasonable precautions to avoid pollution like check:

  • spreading equipment for leaks and correct calibration
  • the organic manure content in and moisture levels of the soil - you can use the Nutrient Management Guide RB209 on the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board website or other resources to help you

Try to incorporate fertiliser into the soil within 12 hours or as soon as possible. This does not apply to grassland.

Keep records

You must record:

  • what you’re putting on - you can keep a product label or invoice
  • where you apply the fertiliser
  • risk factors in that area
  • what you’ve done to reduce the risk

Recommended actions

Record:

  • when you applied fertiliser
  • weather conditions at the time
