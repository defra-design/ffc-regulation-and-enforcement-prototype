Legal requirements

When you apply manufactured fertiliser, you must:

  • have a plan for your application
  • take into account the weather conditions and forecasts at the time
  • take reasonable precautions to avoid significant risk of pollution

You must not apply manufactured fertiliser:

  • if soil is waterlogged, flooded, or snow covered
  • if soil has been frozen for more than 12 hours in the previous 24 hours
  • if there is significant risk of causing pollution
  • within 2 metres of inland freshwaters, coastal waters, a spring, well or borehole

Best practice when you apply fertiliser

Before you apply fertiliser, you should take reasonable precautions to avoid pollution like check:

  • spreading equipment for leaks and correct calibration
  • the organic manure content in and moisture levels of the soil

Try to incorporate fertiliser into the soil within 12 hours or as soon as possible. This does not apply to grassland.
